Hijama (Cupping Therapy)

Traditional Islamic Medicine for Modern Wellness

Hijama, also known as wet cupping, is an ancient healing practice rooted in Islamic tradition. This therapeutic technique involves creating suction on specific points of the body to draw out stagnant blood and toxins, promoting natural healing and improved circulation. Our certified practitioners use sterile, single-use equipment following strict hygiene protocols.

✨ Key Benefits

  • Detoxifies blood and removes toxins
  • Improves blood circulation
  • Relieves chronic pain and inflammation
  • Boosts immune system function
  • Reduces stress and promotes relaxation
  • Treats migraines and headaches
  • Supports fertility and hormonal balance

📋 The Process

  1. Consultation to discuss health concerns
  2. Identification of optimal cupping points
  3. Sterilization of treatment area
  4. Application of cups with suction
  5. Small superficial incisions (wet cupping)
  6. Extraction of stagnant blood
  7. Wound care and aftercare guidance

Dry Cupping Therapy

Non-Invasive Healing Through Suction

Dry cupping uses suction cups without incisions to stimulate blood flow and promote healing. This gentler alternative to wet cupping is ideal for those seeking the benefits of cupping therapy without bloodletting. Perfect for pain management, relaxation, and muscle recovery.

✨ Key Benefits

  • Increases blood circulation
  • Reduces muscle tension and knots
  • Alleviates back and neck pain
  • Promotes lymphatic drainage
  • Reduces inflammation
  • Improves skin health

📋 The Process

  2. Selection of treatment points

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hijama painful?
Most clients report minimal discomfort during Hijama. The sensation is often described as a slight pinching feeling during incision, followed by relief as toxins are removed. We work at your comfort level throughout the procedure.
How often should I get treatment?
Treatment frequency varies based on individual needs. For general wellness, monthly sessions are recommended. For specific conditions, more frequent sessions may be beneficial initially. We'll create a personalized schedule during your consultation.
Are there any side effects?
Minor side effects may include temporary circular marks, slight soreness, or bruising at treatment sites. These typically fade within a few days. Serious complications are rare when performed by trained practitioners with proper hygiene protocols.
Who should avoid these treatments?
Cupping therapy is not recommended for pregnant women, people with bleeding disorders, those on blood thinners, or individuals with skin infections at treatment sites. We conduct thorough consultations to ensure safety for all clients.
